The Science of Immutable Proof

The Architecture of Trust

In the digital age, information is fluid. Files can be altered, timestamps can be backdated, and digital records can be deleted with a single keystroke. This inherent volatility creates a "Digital Dilemma": how can one prove, with absolute certainty, that a specific document, contract, or creative work existed in a specific state at a specific point in history?

The answer lies in the convergence of One-Way Cryptographic Hashing and blockchain technologies. While various decentralized ledgers exist, our service utilizes the Bitcoin Blockchain—the most widely adopted and computationally secure ledger in history—to provide the ultimate anchor for your data.

Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

This service is made possible by the tireless work of the global open-source community. We wish to pay special tribute to the OpenTimestamps (OTS) project. Their pioneering work has provided the world with a standard for trust that is open, free, and independent of any single corporate entity.

By leveraging the OTS protocol, we are able to offer this notarization service entirely for free. We believe that the ability to prove the existence of information is a fundamental digital right.

The Merkle Tree & Scalability

To maintain efficiency, we utilize a Merkle Tree structure. Rather than attempting to record every individual user hash directly on-chain, we "merge" thousands of hashes into a single mathematical summary known as a Merkle Root.

The Bitcoin blockchain records only the final Root, while your .ots file contains the specific "Merkle Path" required to prove that your individual hash was a constituent part of that Root.

Practical Applications

Case Study I: Intellectual Property Safeguarding

Before filing a patent, generating a Proof of Existence for your research papers or code creates a "Digital Seal" of possession. Should a dispute arise, your Bitcoin-anchored timestamp serves as indisputable evidence of prior art.

Case Study II: Legal Integrity & Chain of Custody

By notarizing a document at the moment of signing, you ensure that any future attempt to alter the text will result in a hash mismatch, proving the version being viewed is identical to the original.

Verification Protocol

  1. Original Asset: Retain your original, unaltered file.
  2. The Proof: Use the .ots file delivered to your email.
  3. Independent Verifier: Visit OpenTimestamps.org.
  4. Mathematical Match: Drag both files into the verifier to confirm the anchor in a specific Bitcoin Block.

Custodial Responsibility

NOTICE: This is a non-custodial service. You are the sole custodian of your evidence. You must store the original file and the proof together. If either is lost, the notarization becomes mathematically impossible to verify.
